Strong, Emphasis, Blockquote and Quotes

Strong, < strong > will be rendered like < b >. When in doubt, remember that < strong > should be used for things like warnings, where < b > draws attention to content.

Emphasis, < em > will look like italics. But it shouldn't be used for that purpose. < em > should be used when you want to stress a word. If you go back to lesson 5, you'll see me make a mistake with the phrase 'delicate sixfold symmetry'. In my next example, I'm not using < em > ... you may want to view source.

Blockquote, < blockquote > is used for longer quotes. It allows you to indent your text. I'm going to crib from Sloan's "Underwhelmed":

She was underwhelmed, if that's a word
I know it's not, 'cause I looked it up
That's one of those skills that I learned in my school

I was overwhelmed, and I'm sure of that one
'cause I learned it back in grade school
When I was young

She said, "You is funny"
I said, "You are funny"
She said, "Thank you"
and I said, "Nevermind"

Quote, < q > is used for short quotes. Browsers should add quotes around your text.
